Urban Research Analyst (Urban Development)

Job Title:
Urban Research Analyst (Urban Development)
Application Deadline:
July 13, 2018
Contract Duration
July 9th 2018 - December 21st 2018
Location
OpenNorth Office, Montreal, QC

Full-time, 37.5 hours a week

Do you want to make a difference in Canadian and international public policy? Are you passionate about digital technology and the future of urban development? Want to be part of a dynamic team in the fast-growing field of open government and smart cities?

OpenNorth is Canada’s leading not-for-profit organization specialized in open data, open government, community engagement, smart cities and civic technology. We use open data to promote government transparency and accountability while boosting the effectiveness of community social movements and initiatives.

OpenNorth is looking for an Urban Research Analyst (Urban Development)

The Analyst will assume a hands-on role in a research team and be responsible for supporting and delivering various research projects in the realm of open data, open government, and much more. These projects pertain to issues of citizen engagement, public policy, spatial data, and data standardisation, at the municipal level. Projects are both local and international.

Directed by the OpenNorth team, the analyst will complete primary research (including academic and grey/mainstream literature) on topics including, but not limited to:

  • Open data and open government
  • Smart cities
  • Data standards
  • Urban resilience and systems

Responsibilities:

  • Research and coordination support for multiple, concurrent projects
  • Perform environmental scans on select topics to monitor trends and cutting edge developments
  • Provide written, comprehensive summaries on select topics
  • Provide critical analysis on current and future developments in given topics, and draw links to topical issues
  • Attend project meetings and coordinate transcripts for internal research communications
  • Conduct interviews and support the design of research methodology
  • Contribute to OpenNorth reports and presentations
  • Report findings to the OpenNorth team
  • Plan and coordinate meetings with local stakeholders (French is a requirement)

Your Profile:

  • Passionate about cities and the use of tech and data to explore urban environment; stays up to date on topics such as: mobility, food security, systems of resource sharing, collaborative planning
  • Affinity for variety of research materials including technical reports containing quantitative or qualitative data analysis, academic literature, and government policy
  • Resourceful, autonomous worker with strong initiative and self-motivation
  • Critical thinker who draws links between different concepts
  • Strong grounding in geography or urban planning theory
  • Strong understanding of municipal governance structure

Qualifications

  • Strong communication skills, both verbal and written
  • Fluent in English and French (excellent written and verbal English, very strong written and verbal French for communication)
  • Other languages are an asset
  • Strong reporting abilities, experience writing concise and comprehensive summaries
  • Experience coordinating communications across multiple groups of people or projects
  • Demonstrable experience with spatial analysis and using GIS

What We Offer

  • Opportunity to travel to conduct field research in Canada and internationally
  • Opportunity to work with leading researchers across Canada and internationally
  • A stimulating and fast-paced work environment
  • Sunny office full of plants located in Mile-End, and a desk to call your own

How do I apply?

Submit your resume (maximum 2 pages), a cover letter (1 page), and a minimum of 2 writing samples.

Please submit your application to: jobs@opennorth.ca

(Note: incomplete applications will not be considered)

We encourage people from various fields to apply including, but not limited to, academia, research institutions, private sector, not-for-profits, think tanks, journalism and international organizations. We look forward to hearing from you!

About OpenNorth

Founded in 2011, OpenNorth is Canada’s leading not-for-profit organization specialized in open data, open government, community engagement, smart cities and civic technology. We are an active member of a global community working in favour of systems change, improving government transparency and accountability, and increasing public participation in democracy. Guided by our core values, we enhance data standards, foster community-building, cultivate partnerships and improve democracy. Our work leads to collaborations with all level of government in Canada, as well as globally with international organizations and networks.
